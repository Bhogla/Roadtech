# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Technical B2B buyers in Indian road infrastructure: highway contractors, NHAI/PWD/state-PWD procurement engineers, distributors, and applicator associates. They arrive evaluating a supplier — looking for proof of capability (plants, lab, R&D), the right product grade for a spec (e.g. RS-1 tack coat, CRMB 60), and a way to reach the technical team. They read datasheets for a living; they distrust vague marketing and inflated claims. Often on desktop in an office, sometimes on mobile from a site.

## Product Purpose
A rebuild of roadtech-asphalt.com for Roadtech Asphalt Technologies Pvt Ltd — a manufacturer of bitumen emulsions, modified bitumen, cold mix products, and road-maintenance services (microsurfacing, slurry seal) for highways, expressways, runways, and race/test tracks in India (incorporated Jan 2020). The site must communicate manufacturing credibility, present an honest product catalog, and convert qualified buyers into a contact/enquiry. Success = a procurement engineer can find the right grade and reach the technical team in under a minute, trusting that what's shown is real.

## Brand Personality
Industrial, precise, credible. Voice is plain and technical, not salesy — the tone of an engineering spec sheet written by people who actually run the plant. Three words: **engineered, grounded, exacting.** Should evoke confidence and competence, never hype. The unifying device is "datasheet language": mono grade codes, bracketed figure labels, label/value pairs — the visual grammar of the documents this audience already trusts.

## Anti-references
- Generic AI-generated B2B SaaS landing page (cream background, soft gradients, rounded icon-cards, tracked-uppercase eyebrow above every section).
- The legacy site's failure modes: literal "Upload Image…" placeholders, dead `#` links, garbled vision copy, invented or absent specs.
- Stocky corporate-construction clip-art and hard-hat-handshake imagery.
- Inflated trust signals — fabricated certificate numbers, made-up stats, specs we don't have.

## Design Principles
1. **Show the real thing.** Real Indian-highway and plant imagery, real grade codes, real addresses. No fabricated specs or certifications — where content is genuinely missing, say "available on request," never invent.
2. **Datasheet over brochure.** The mono/label-value/figure-tag system is the through-line. Technical legibility is the aesthetic.
3. **Earned restraint, committed contrast.** Orange is a scalpel, not a highlighter. Power comes from the dark/warm rhythm and typographic scale, not decoration.
4. **Honesty as a feature.** Gaps are handled with dignified empty states and "contact our technical team," which reads as more credible to this audience than padded copy.
5. **Spec-sheet legibility everywhere.** Strong contrast, keyboard focus, semantic structure — this audience scans and verifies.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 (note: the muted `aggregate` gray is for labels/borders only, never body copy on warm backgrounds). Visible keyboard focus on every interactive element. Semantic HTML, labelled form fields and nav, accessible tab/accordion patterns. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for all scroll/entrance motion. Responsive and legible mobile→desktop.
